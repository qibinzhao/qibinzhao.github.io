Modern machine learning technologies often demand vast amounts of data, large-scale models, and considerable computational resources, which also results in challenges related to the reliability and interpretability of well-trained models. Our team aims to develope efficient, robust, and interpretable machine learning models and algorithms, along with their theoretical analysis. Our research spans various areas such as self-supervised learning, unsupervised representation learning, multi-modal learning and deep generative models. We focus on several key directions including tensor methods for machine learning, robust and interpretable machine learning and quantum machine learning. We also seek collaborations in practical domains such as brain computer interfaces, remote sensing, medical diagnosis and quantum computing.

Research Topics:

  • ■ Tensor Methods for Machine Learning

    Our research lies in the intersection of tensor networks and machine learning. We investigate tensor network theory with its algorithms and leverage tensor network representations to address critical challenges in deep learning, such as data and parameter efficiency, adversarial robustness and interpretability of deep generative models, as well as the theoretical understanding of tensor-based learning methods.

  • ■ Trustworthy Machine Learning

    Our research focuses on quantum-inspired machine learning models and hybrid quantum-classical algorithms, aiming to harness quantum advantages to enhance machine learning technologies. We intend to investigate tensor networks for data encoding, quantum circuit compression, and quantum algorithms. This research is supported by the RIKEN Quantum project.

  • ■Quantum Machine Learning

    Our research focuses on quantum-inspired machine learning models and hybrid quantum-classical algorithms, aiming to harness quantum advantages to enhance machine learning technologies. We intend to investigate tensor networks for data encoding, quantum circuit compression, and quantum algorithms. This research is supported by the RIKEN Quantum project.
